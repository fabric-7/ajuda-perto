# Local Chapters

Local chapters are groups responsible for mapping, verifying, and maintaining data for a city, region, or country.

## Objective

Allow communities to care for their own data, languages, priorities, and partnerships while keeping shared standards for privacy, security, and quality.

## Requirements to Create a Chapter

A local chapter must have:

- at least two responsible people;
- a defined city or region;
- clear initial categories;
- a verification process;
- a contact channel;
- commitment to the project's central documents.

## Responsibilities

- Map local services.
- Verify sources.
- Correct outdated data.
- Respond to reports.
- Prioritize critical services.
- Support translation and local language.
- Build relationships with real organizations.

## Autonomy

Chapters may adapt categories, languages, and local priorities.

They may not weaken privacy, security, AI, funding, or conduct rules.

## Recommended Expansion

Start with:

- one city;
- a few categories;
- 100 to 300 services;
- weekly review;
- partnerships with local organizations.

Then replicate the method.
